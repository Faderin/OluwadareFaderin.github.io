We advocate for equal birthing method rights

We advocate for equal birthing method rights and educate women on Cesarean delivery (C-section) as a way of reducing risk of physical and psychological injury to the baby compared to a 'difficult' vaginal birth.

Why C-Section Mum Initiative!

C-section Mum Initiative is a non-governmental organization that advocates the reduction of maternal and neonatal mortality rates in Nigeria. In the past three years, we have grown by 80%. We attribute the growth to our commitment to educating and providing financial and social support for mums.

First trimester: key stages

The first trimester begins on the first day of your last period and lasts until the end of week 12. This means that by the time you know for sure you're pregnant, you might already be five or six weeks pregnant! A lot happens during these first three months. The fertilised egg rapidly divides into layers of cells and implants in the wall of your womb where it carries on growing. These layers of cells become an embryo, which is what the baby is called at this stage. During this trimester, your baby grows faster than at any other time. By six weeks, a heartbeat can usually be heard and by the end of week 12, your baby's bones, muscles and all the organs of the body have formed. At this point, your baby looks like a tiny human being and is now called a fetus. He or she will even be practising swallowing!
